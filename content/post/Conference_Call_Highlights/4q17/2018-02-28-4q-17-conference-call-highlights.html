Service Companies


Halliburton

  • Total company revenue was $5.9 billion, representing a 9% increase from 3Q’17.
  • North American revenue was up ~7% sequentially. Improvement was driven by increased utilization and pricing throughout the United States.
  • Highlighted select technology:
    • Geometrix 4D Shaped Cutters - Improve cutting efficiency and increase control to reduce drilling costs.
    • JetPulse - Provides high speed data transmission of drilling and formation evaluation measures.
    • Marine Sentry - Device providing pressure control solutions by creating a seal around the drill string and tool joints for safer containment of fluids.
    • BaraShale - Water-based fluid designed to maintain full salt saturation with reduced density, which should help prevent lost circulation and minimize disposal costs.
    • Electromagnetic Pipe Xaminer - Allows operators to pinpoint casing defect and metal corrosion in up to five tubular strings within the well.
  • Have seen cost inflation in sand and trucking. Price of sand has escalated over the last few months of 2017. However, increased sand supply from localized mines should reduce costs in 2018.
  • Completions market remains tight and is currently sold out; will continue to push pricing throughout the year.
  • Rig to frac spread ratio is ~2:1. During the oil price decline, the spread rose to as high as ~4:1.
    • Industry horsepower is working harder, which is increasing pace of degradation on equipment.
  • Believe price inflation could be higher than 15% in 2018.

Helmerich and Payne

  • Average spot pricing still in the high-teens, while leading edge Super-Spec FlexRig pricing is in the low-to-mid $20s.
    • Super-Spec rigs include AC drive with 1,500 hp drawworks, 750,000# hookload ratings, 7,500 psi mud circulating systems and multiple well pad drilling systems.
  • Industry is transitioning to Super-Spec rigs as the market share has increased from ~34% to ~40% in 7 months.
  • Company has 102 rigs contracted in the Permian. Represents ~50% of total company contracts.
  • As of January 2018, over 70% of total horizontal activity is utilizing AC Drive rigs.
  • Highlighted MOTIVE and MagVAR capabilities:
    • MOTIVE: Bit guidance system which has been used on more than 350 horizontal wells to date. Allows for more accurate well placement, smoother wellbores, more efficient drilling with consistently lower well costs.
      • Eagle Ford case study: 2 sets of 4 wells (one guided by MOTIVE and one without), with nearly identical lithologies, well paths etc. MOTIVE provided 30% lower effective drilling cost with nearly twice the accuracy and zero non-productive time.

    • Medusa: 13 well pattern across 3 intervals in the Leonard, Bone Spring.

    • Snapping: 6 well pattern across 2 intervals in the Leonard.


  • In the Rattlesnake Area (Loving/Lea border), the Seawolf project is testing 28 wells per section from a 12 well pattern consisting of a single Third Bone Spring, 4 Wolfcamp XY, 5 Wolfcamp A Upper and a single Middle and Lower Wolfcamp A.

    • 7 of its 10 MDPs have been submitted to regulatory agencies. MDPs are designed to accommodate ~1,600 drilling permits.
    • 4 MDPs have been approved.
    • Management provided commentary on the permitting process stating, “the overall plan is so that we can get permits not just on subsurface, but surface right-of-way, takeaway, battery, et cetera. So what we can do is cover the NEPA, the surface area all at one time. In doing this, it helps the BLM to be able to do more permits in a timely manner. So we work very closely with them, have a very good relationship. Once you have that approved, it then works on to the APD. Upon approval, the commitment from the BLM by statute is 30 days as after we get those approved.”

    • Running 16 rigs and 5 frac crews.
  • Reserves totaled 416 MMboe, which represents a 87% increase from 2016.
  • Guiding 2018 production to increase by 28% to an average daily rate of 98-108 Mboe/d (65-70 Mbo/d) with an associated capital budget of $1.35-$1.55 billion.
