Service Companies


Halliburton

  • Total company revenue was $5.9 billion, representing a 9% increase from 3Q’17.
  • North American revenue was up ~7% sequentially. Improvement was driven by increased utilization and pricing throughout the United States.
  • Highlighted select technology:
    • Geometrix 4D Shaped Cutters - Improve cutting efficiency and increase control to reduce drilling costs.
    • JetPulse - Provides high speed data transmission of drilling and formation evaluation measures.
    • Marine Sentry - Device providing pressure control solutions by creating a seal around the drill string and tool joints for safer containment of fluids.
    • BaraShale - Water-based fluid designed to maintain full salt saturation with reduced density, which should help prevent lost circulation and minimize disposal costs.
    • Electromagnetic Pipe Xaminer - Allows operators to pinpoint casing defect and metal corrosion in up to five tubular strings within the well.
  • Have seen cost inflation in sand and trucking. Price of sand has escalated over the last few months of 2017. However, increased sand supply from localized mines should reduce costs in 2018.
  • Completions market remains tight and is currently sold out; will continue to push pricing throughout the year.
  • Rig to frac spread ratio is ~2:1. During the oil price decline, the spread rose to as high as ~4:1.
    • Industry horsepower is working harder, which is increasing pace of degradation on equipment.
  • Believe price inflation could be higher than 15% in 2018.

Upstream Companies


Anadarko

  • Onshore production averaged 391 Mboe/d, which was an increase of 9% from 4Q’16.
    • Delaware Basin production increased 54% to average 77 Mboe/d (61% oil).
  • Company estimates there are over 10,000 drilling locations and over 4 BBoe in net resource across it’s 589,000 gross (240,00 net) acre position.
    • Wolfcamp A accounts for 3 BBoe of the total.
  • Currently running 9 rigs and 7 frac crews in the Delaware.

  • Expect to PoP 160 wells and deliver YoY production growth of more than 50% in the Delaware.

  • Implemented extended flow back program on 30 wells across its acreage footprint with results exceeding 2017 Wolfcamp A type curve by 25%.
    • Seeing increased gains on unconstrained flowback due to buildout of oil treating facilities.
    • By YE18, Anadarko midstream and Western Gas plan to place into service an additional 400 MMcf/d of gas processing and 120 Mbo/d of oil treating capacity.
    • Management believes infrastructure buildout is crucial to outperformance within the basin.


  • Spent $400 million in “other onshore assets” outside of the company’s core acreage. No additional details were provided on where capital was deployed.
  • Hedged ~50% of 2018 production.

Cimarex Energy

  • 2017 production averaged 190 Mboe/d (30 % oil).
  • Guiding CAPEX to $1.6-$1.7 billion ($1.3-$1.4 billion D&C) with associated production of 211-221 Mboe/d, an increase of ~14% from 2017.
    • Permian will receive ~70% of capital.
    • Plan on completing 127 wells total and 84 wells in the Permian.
    • Focus in Permian will continue to be on Wolfcamp XL laterals with additional development in the Avalon and Second Bone Spring.
    • Testing 14 wells per section in the Lower Wolfcamp in Culberson and 18 wells per section in the Upper Wolfcamp in Reeves.
  • Currently running 10 rigs in the Permian.
  • Proppant costs have risen by ~10% since the third quarter.
    • Countering rising prices by deploying zipper fracs and recycling water.
    • Continue to explore local sand sourcing.
  • Wolfcamp 2-mile D&C costs are ~$11-$13 million.
  • Bone Spring 1-mile D&C costs are ~$7-$8.5 million.

  • Multiple pilot spacing tests are currently producing or in development

  • In Culberson, the Tim Tam development project generated >67% IRR after taxes.
    • Wells were wine racked in the Lower Wolfcamp with horizontal spacing of 1,756’ and vertical spacing of 200’.
    • Spacing appears to be adequate; child wells are outperforming parent wells after one year.

  • In Red Hills, the Red Tank Triste Draw development is testing 20 wells per section in the Avalon.

  • Testing X-Y Bench in Culberson. Multiple wells are currently flowing back with published results expected within the next few quarters.
    • Positive results would push play farther West into State Line area.
  • Increasing activity in Ward. Gaining more confidence landing laterals in Upper Wolfcamp without detrimental effect to the upper Third Bone Spring wells.
    • Company will drill 4-5 wells across it’s acreage in 2018.
  • Possibly moving away from building large scale infrastructure in front of multi-pad developments and shifting back to smaller facilities with a “build it as you need it” mentality.
    • Read through: Front-end loading is negatively affecting project IRRs.

Chevron

  • Replaced 155% of reserves primarily driven by Permian, Gorgon and GoM.
  • Fourth quarter production from the Permian was 205 Mboe/d.
  • Operated 16 rigs and 6 frac fleets.
  • Transacted more than ~60,000 acres in the Midland and Delaware Basins adding 600 XL laterals to inventory.
  • Multiple deals expected to close in 2018.
  • Using technology to better assess seismic attributes to control drill bits and improve precision of lateral landing.
  • Increased number of stages and lowered perforated intervals.
  • Around two-thirds of capital spend in the Permian is protected with contracts.

ConocoPhillips

  • Estimate full-year production guidance of 1,215 Mboe/d and capex of $5.5 billion.
    • 4Q’17 Lower 48 production averaged 236 Mboe/d.
  • Increased dividend by ~7.5% and planned share buybacks by 33%.
  • Company strategy unchanged with focus on free cash flow and funding the dividend.
  • Acquired 245,000 net acres of unconventional exploration leases for $235 million in three different early-stage plays.
    • Did not divulge further details as company is still coring up position.
    • Acreage is being acquired at less than $1,000 per acre.
  • Permian acreage transacting over $30,000/acre is not competitive in current portfolio.

Diamondback Energy

  • 2017 production averaged 79.2 Mboe/d (74% oil), an increase of 84% from the previous year.
  • Proved reserves totaled 335.4 MMboe (62% PDP, 70% oil), up 63% YoY.
  • Guiding production 108-116 Mboe/d, an increase of 40% from the midpoint of 2017.
  • CAPEX expected to be $1.3-$1.5 billion; infrastructure represents ~11% of total expenditures.
    • Plan to PoP 170-190 gross wells with an average lateral length of 9,300’.
  • Initiating dividend of $0.50 starting in the first quarter of 2018.
    • Read through: Although the distribution amount is quite small, Diamondback is highlighting its cash generation capability while also separating itself from other peers.
  • Operated 10 rigs and 4 frac crews in the fourth quarter. Plan on operating 10-12 rigs throughout 2018.
    • Completed 19 Lower Spraberry, 15 Wolfcamp A and 4 Wolfcamp B wells.

  • Announced ~19,000 net acre exploratory play located in Southeast Ector and Northeast Andrews Counties.
    • Initially targeting Mississippian Barnett, which they say is analogous to recent successful Mississippian activity in Andrews County.
    • Appraisal of acreage will begin in 2018.
    • Private operators, Zarvona and Elevation are active in Andrews County.
      • Zarvona Energy is led by CEO Kathryn Macaskie (former SVP at Enervest) and backed by Salient.
      • Elevation Resources is led by Steve Pruett (ex-Concho) and backed by Pine Brook Partners.
  • Current inventory count across Midland and Delaware Basins is ~3,800 gross locations at current strip pricing.
    • In the Midland Basin, reduced spacing in the Wolfcamp A and Wolfcamp B from 6 to 7 wells per section.


  • Corporate model assumes service cost inflation of ~12% in the Midland Basin and ~17% in the Delaware Basin.
Delaware Basin
  • Running 4 rigs and 2 frac crews and expect to average 4-5 rigs and 2+ frac cres in 2018.
  • Completed it’s first two well pad targeting the Wolfcamp A and Wolfcamp B in Reeves County.
    • Ayers 24-2WA (7,829’ lateral) achieved a peak 30-day IP rate of 226 Boe/d per 1,000’ or 1,769 Boe/d (82% oil).
    • Ayers 24-3WB (8,801’ lateral) achieved a peak 30-day IP rate of 142 Boe/d per 1,000’ or 1,250 Boe/d (81% oil).
      • 90 day cumulative production is over 95 Mboe.
    • Did not see any communication between the formations; management believes at this time there is no need to co-develop the zones.
  • 2018 activity will primarily focus on Wolfcamp A in Reeves, Ward and Pecos.

  • Plan on additional tests of Second Bone Spring in Pecos and Wolfcamp B and Third Bone Spring in Reeves and Ward.

  • Multiple wells have reached TD in less than 20 days.
    • Utilizing pre-set rig and testing different hole sizes surface depths and intermediate casing points for further improvement.
  • Current completion design using 2,000-2,500 lbs/ft with tighter stage spacing.
    • Continue to test different fluid rates, diverting agents and cluster spacing.

  • Highgrading Wolfcamp landing zones with high resolution 3-D seismic shoot in 2018.
    • Targeting three different landing zones in the Wolfcamp which is estimated to hold 53 MMbls per section of OOIP.

  • Recent Wolfcamp A well results are tracking 1,100 Mboe (970 Mbo) type curve.

  • Water cuts are around 4:1 in Pecos and Reeves Counties.

Midland Basin
  • Running 6 rigs and expect to average 6-8 rigs in 2018.
  • 2018 activity will focus on Lower Spraberry in Western Midland Basin and Wolfcamp A in Eastern Midland Basin.
  • Current completion design using 1,600-1,800 lbs/ft and 100% local sand.

ExxonMobil

  • 2017 production averaged 3,985 Mboe/d, 2% down from 2016.
  • Plan to spend $50 billion in the US over the next five years with a majority going in the Permian.
  • Increasing rig count to 30 rigs in the Permian by YE2018.
  • Recently began producing from its first Delaware 12,500’ lateral.
  • Early results are in line with models.
  • Continue to assess multi-zone potential across Delaware and Midland Basins.

Laredo Petroleum

  • 2017 production averaged 58 Mboe/d, up 17% from 2016.
  • Increased reserves by 36%.
  • Reiterated 2018 growth guidance of ~10% versus last year.
  • Announced a $200 million share repurchase program. Funds will be sourced from cash on hand of ~$46 million and its revolving credit facility.
    • Management stated, “At current stock prices, we believe having the ability to repurchase 10% of our outstanding shares represents a highly accretive use of capital.”
    • Read through: Laredo is a small cap company with growth rates similar to that of a mid-major. Distributing capital through share buybacks make sense given the company profile.
  • Currently running 3 rigs and 1 frac crew.
    • Adding another crew in the second quarter.
    • Plan on completing 60-65 net wells targeting the Upper and Middle Wolfcamp with an average latera length of ~10,400’.
  • Expect to achieve well cost savings of ~$600k per well compared to 2017 D&C costs.
    • Decrease attributed to longer stage lengths, local sand and other completion design changes.
  • Results of spacing tests in 2017 suggest the possibility for up to 32 wells per section in the Upper and Middle Wolfcamp.
    • ~60% of wells in 2018 will be drilled using this spacing assumption.

Murphy Oil

  • 2018 production expected to be ~168 Mboe/d with associated capex of $1.06 billion.
  • Permian CAPEX estimated to be ~$16.5 million.
  • In process of testing both contiguous acreage positions in Dawson and Andrews Counties.
    • Two wells are currently online and plan on drilling additional two wells in 2018.

Occidental Petroleum

  • First off, Oxy receives 2 blue ribbons for both the longest deck (64 slides) and most words crammed into each slide…now on to the commentary.
  • Permian Resources 2017/2018 operating results and guidance:
    • Production in the fourth quarter averaged 159 Mboe/d.
    • Replaced 365% of reserves.
    • Added 750 locations which breakeven <$50.
    • 2018 production expected to be 169-173 Mboe/d with associated CAPEX of ~$1.9 billion.
    • Plan on running 11 rigs in 2018.
      • 10 rigs in Delaware and 1 rig in Midland.
    • Service costs expected to increase by 5% for drilling and 10%-15% for completions in 2018.

  • Testing 6 new benches in the Delaware Basin which include the First/Second Bone Spring and Avalon in New Mexico and the Second/Third Bone Spring and Wolfcamp C in Greater Barilla Draw.

  • Targeting $8.9 million (10,000’ lateral) Second Bone Spring D&C costs in New Mexico, a reduction of $1 million from fourth quarter average well costs.
    • D&C costs assume 3-string casing and 2,000 lbs/ft completion as well as tie-in capital.
    • Corral Fly 02-01 State 23H (9,800’ lateral): drilled first time with 5-1/2" floated casing.
  • Highlighted Turkey Track development consisting of 6 sections in New Mexico.
    • Targeting Second/Third Bone Spring development with all-in IRRs above 40%.
    • Well costs are ~$7.7 million (10,000’ lateral) drilled in 20 days.
    • Turkey Track 9-10 state 32H completed in the Third Bone Spring has produced over 200 Mboe in 90 days.
    • Placed 5 wells online during the fourth quarter.
    • Delayed moving rig away from Turkey Track due to Third Bone Spring result.
    • Focusing on co-development of the Second/Third Bone Spring in 2018.


  • In the Greater Sand Dunes Region, PoP’d the Patton MDP1 18 Fed23H which is an Avalon appraisal well that had an IP30 of 1,509 Be/d (76% oil).
  • Also in the Greater Sand Dunes Region, testing new frac designs and should have results in early 2018.
  • In the Midland Basin, operating expenses in Merchant averaged ~$2.71 in 2017 driven largely by centralized facilities, no water hauling and using gas lift which has limited well failures and downhole costs.
  • Big data analytics (buzz buzz) reducing workovers through early detection of problems in artificial lifts and drilling.


Pioneer Natural Resources

  • Divesting Eagle Ford, South Texas, Raton and West Panhandle assets in 2018 to become Permian Basin pure player.
    • Post divestitures, cash operating margins and corporate returns expected to be significantly higher.
  • 2017 production averaged 207 Mboe/d, which was ~16% higher than 2016.
  • Replaced 309% of reserves (314 MMboe) at an average F&D cost of $8.46 per BOE.
  • PoP’d 64 horizontal wells during the fourth quarter, 8 wells utilized Version 3.0+ completions.
  • Permian Basin 2018 forecast production expected to grow by 19%-24%.
    • Oil production expected to grow by similar percentage.
  • 2018 Capex is ~$2.9 billion with more than 90% towards D&C.
    • Assumes 5% cost inflation.
    • Vertical integration should alleviate impact of 10%-15% cost inflation forecasted across industry.
  • Plan on operating 20 rigs and PoP 250-275 wells in 2018.
    • 16 rigs in the northern portion.
    • 4 rigs in the Wolfcamp JV area.
  • Version 3.0+ results look encouraging based on early time data.
    • Utilizing between 2,500-5,000 lbs/ft depending on area.
    • Proppant loading is significantly higher than industry average of ~2,000 lbs/ft in the Midland Basin.
    • IRRs averaging 65% assuming $55/Bbl and $3/Mcf.


  • First Wolfcamp D with Version 3.0 completion PoP’d in Midland County has produced 120 Mboe/d (72% oil) in 45 days.
    • Completing three additional wells with higher proppant loading in 2018.
  • Company estimates Wolfcamp D prospective on ~450,000 gross acres.


  • Appraisal wells mainly targeting Middle and Lower Spraberry on northern portion of acreage next year.
    • 10 wells will test Jo Mill and Middle Spraberry.
    • 9 wells will test Lower Spraberry
  • Plan on drilling first Clearfork well in Midland County.
  • Reducing 4-string casing design to ~50% of program from ~75% in 2H2017.
  • Updated EURs and D&C costs for Wolfcamp A/B and Spraberry Intervals from third quarter.
    • Wolfcamp B costs increased by $100k and EURs remained unchanged.
    • Wolfcamp A costs increased by $500k and EURs increased by 100 Mboe.
    • Spraberry costs and EURs remained unchanged.

  • Permian infrastructure build out underway spending ~$300 million for SWD facilities and Below-Grade Cellars.
    • Implementing Below-Cellars on 24-well pads which minimizes future surface acreage requirements and reduces full-cycle surface costs per well.
    • Expect to reuse 15%-20% of produced water in 2018.
  • Plan on saving around 20% on sand using local mines.
    • Majority of sand composed of finer mesh sizes (100 and 40/70).
    • In process of testing multiple pilot projects to ensure there is no degradation of well results.
  • More than 85% of forecasted 2018 Permian oil production hedged.
    • Hedging percentage considerably higher than peer average of ~45%. See the link (Permian Basin Hedging) for a review of hedging profiles from our peers.
  • 2018 cash flow breakeven oil price is ~$58 based on all in horizontal cost structure of $19/Boe.


Shell

  • Sticking to capital range between $25-$30 billion. through 2020.
    • Hard ceiling even in higher price environment.
  • Reduced debt by $8 billion dollars over the previous four quarters.
    • Gearing stands at ~24.8%.
  • Corporate strategy remains focused on increasing shareholder distributions.
  • Awarded 9 blocks in the GoM through the Mexican open bidding process.
    • Previously announced the Whale deep discovery (60% WI) which encountered more than 1,400’ of net oil bearing pay.
  • Management would consider reducing growth in Permian if necessary.
    • Reading through the lines, Shell might be open to asset divestitures in it’s unconventional assets given upcoming major project decisions at Vito in GoM, LNG Canada and its other long term assets.

Oil and Gas Producer Pricing Index

 Feb 2, 2018
Producer Pricing Index What does it measure? The Producer Price Index (PPI) program measures the average change over time in the selling prices received by domestic producers for their output. The prices included in the PPI are from the first commercial transaction for many products and some services. Why is it useful? Commonly used in escalating purchase and sales contracts. Indicator of overall price movement at the producer level.
